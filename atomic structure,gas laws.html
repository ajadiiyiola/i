    Brownian Motion Simulation

    Watch the large particle get bombarded randomly by tiny invisible molecules — just like pollen in water!

    Brownian Motion — Live View
    Temperature / Molecular Speed Medium
    Concept Explained
    What you see:
    • GOLD dot = Large visible particle (e.g. pollen grain, smoke particle)
    • BLUE dots = Tiny invisible molecules
    • ORANGE trail = Path traced by large particle
    Why it moves randomly:
    At any instant, more molecules hit one side than the other. This unequal bombardment pushes the large particle in a random direction — creating the characteristic zigzag path.
    ⚠ JAMB: Brownian motion is DIRECT EVIDENCE that molecules exist and are in continuous random motion. It DOES NOT decrease with time (constant temperature = constant motion).
    Brownian Motion ∝ Temperature
    Increase temperature → faster molecules → more erratic motion

    Combined Gas Law — P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂

    Use when BOTH pressure AND temperature change simultaneously.

    3D Gas Law Visualisation
    When to use each law:
    • T constant → Boyle's: P₁V₁ = P₂V₂
    • P constant → Charles': V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂
    • V constant → Pressure Law: P₁/T₁ = P₂/T₂
    • All change → Combined: P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂

    Graham's Law of Diffusion

    Lighter gases diffuse FASTER. Rate ∝ 1/√M

    r₁/r₂ = √(M₂/M₁)

    SQUARING TRICK: If Gas A is X times faster than Gas B, then:
    M(B) = X² × M(A)
