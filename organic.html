    Cracking & Reforming
    CRACKING: Long-chain hydrocarbons → shorter, more useful molecules + alkenes.
    Thermal cracking: 700–900°C, high pressure, no catalyst.
    Catalytic cracking: 500°C, Al₂O₃/SiO₂ catalyst. More controlled.
    WHY crack? Long chains = poor petrol. Short chains + alkenes = better petrol + plastics material!
    REFORMING: Straight-chain → branched + aromatic compounds → HIGHER OCTANE NUMBER (better petrol quality).
    OCTANE NUMBER: Heptane = 0 (knocks badly). 2,2,4-trimethylpentane (iso-octane) = 100 (best). Higher octane = smoother engine running.
    🧠 Fractions mnemonic: "Really Powerful Naughty Kids Don't Like Flat Bikes" = Refinery gas, Petrol, Naphtha, Kerosene, Diesel, Lubricating oil, Fuel oil, Bitumen — lightest to heaviest!

    Alkanols — The Alcohol Family

    Primary, Secondary, Tertiary — Lucas Test, Fermentation, Production.

    Primary vs Secondary vs Tertiary Alcohols
    The type depends on HOW MANY carbon atoms are bonded to the carbon bearing the —OH group.
    1° = 1 carbon neighbour → Primary
    2° = 2 carbon neighbours → Secondary
    3° = 3 carbon neighbours → Tertiary
    🧠 "1 → Aldehyde, 2 → Ketone, 3 → Nothing!"
    Oxidise primary (1°) → aldehyde → carboxylic acid. Oxidise secondary (2°) → ketone. Tertiary (3°) → cannot be oxidised!
    TypeStructureExampleOxidation Product
    1° PrimaryR—CH₂—OHEthanol CH₃CH₂OHAlkanal → Alkanoic acid
    2° SecondaryR—CHOH—R'Propan-2-ol CH₃CHOHCH₃Alkanone (ketone) only
    3° TertiaryR—C(OH)—R'R''2-Methylpropan-2-olNOT oxidised
    Lucas Test — Distinguish 1°, 2°, 3°
    Lucas reagent = ZnCl₂ + conc. HCl. The alcohol + Lucas reagent → alkyl chloride (cloudy/turbid) if reacts.
    🧠 Lucas Test: "Tertiary Takes off instantly. Secondary Slowly. Primary Patiently waits (no reaction at RT)." Reactivity: 3° > 2° > 1°. Cloudiness = alkyl chloride formed.

    Alkanals vs Alkanones — The Carbonyl Compounds

    Both have C=O — but in DIFFERENT positions. This makes all the difference!

    The KEY Difference
    ALKANAL
    R — CHO
    C=O at the END of chain
    e.g. Ethanal CH₃CHO
    Fehling's → RED ✓
    ALKANONE
    R — CO — R'
    C=O in the MIDDLE of chain
    e.g. Propanone CH₃COCH₃
    Fehling's → BLUE ✗
    🧠 "A before K in the alphabet — Aldehyde Acts (reacts), Ketone Keeps still!"
    A (Aldehyde) comes before K (Ketone) in alphabet. So Aldehyde ACTS first — it reduces Fehling's. Ketone KEEPS the blue colour (no reaction)!

    Esterification — Making Esters
    Acid + Alcohol ⇌ Ester + Water
    Catalyst: conc. H₂SO₄ | Condition: warm gently | REVERSIBLE reaction (⇌)

    Polymers — Natural & Synthetic

    Addition vs Condensation — Thermoplastic vs Thermosetting.

    Polymerisation Types
    ADDITION
    Alkene monomers JOIN by opening C=C double bond.
    NO by-product — all atoms go into polymer.
    Monomers must have C=C bond.
    n(CH₂=CH₂) → [—CH₂—CH₂—]ₙ
    CONDENSATION
    Monomers join with LOSS of H₂O (or HCl) at each step.
    Two different functional groups needed.
    Forms polyamides (nylon), polyesters, etc.
    n(H₂N—R—COOH) → [—NH—R—CO—]ₙ + nH₂O
    🧠 "Addition = ADD nothing else (no loss!). Condensation = CONDENSES water out (H₂O leaves)!"
    Addition keeps EVERYTHING. Condensation throws H₂O away each time monomers join!
    Thermoplastic vs Thermosetting
    THERMOPLASTIC
    ✅ Softens & melts on heating
    ✅ Can be RESHAPED
    ✅ RECYCLABLE
    Structure: linear/branched chains, no cross-links
    Examples: Polythene, PVC, polystyrene, nylon
    THERMOSETTING
    ❌ Does NOT melt (chars/burns)
    ❌ Cannot be reshaped
    ❌ NOT recyclable
    Structure: heavily cross-linked 3D network
    Examples: Bakelite, epoxy resin, melamine
    🧠 "PLASTIC is Pliable (can melt again). SETTING is Set for life!"
    ThermoPLASTIC = can be melted and remoulded (like wax). ThermoSETTING = set forever (like concrete — once hard, always hard)!
